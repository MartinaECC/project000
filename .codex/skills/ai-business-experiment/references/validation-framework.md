# AI Business Experiment Validation Framework

Use this reference when a user wants to evaluate, package, or validate an AI-enabled micro-business idea.

## Opportunity Card

For every idea, fill this card before proposing execution:

| Field | Question |
| --- | --- |
| Customer | Who already pays for a similar result? |
| Pain | What problem is urgent, visible, and easy to explain? |
| Result | What concrete deliverable will the customer receive? |
| AI Leverage | Which step becomes faster, cheaper, or more consistent because of AI? |
| Human Review | What must a human verify before delivery? |
| Channel | Where can the first 5 prospects be reached this week? |
| Price | What is the entry offer, standard package, and monthly package? |
| Risk | What legal, privacy, platform, or quality risks exist? |
| Proof | What evidence would make this worth continuing? |

## Scoring

Score each factor from 1 to 5:

- Pain clarity: the buyer can explain the problem without education.
- Visible output: the deliverable can be inspected quickly.
- Speed to sample: a credible sample can be made within one day.
- Channel access: the first prospects are reachable without a large audience.
- Willingness to pay: the buyer already pays for adjacent work.
- Repeatability: the process can become a checklist, template, or Skill.
- Risk control: copyright, privacy, sensitive advice, and platform rules are manageable.
- Margin: the expected price covers human review and revisions.

Decision:

- 32 or higher: run a 7-day paid validation.
- 24 to 31: narrow the customer or deliverable before testing.
- Below 24: keep as inspiration, do not execute yet.

## 7-Day Validation Plan

Day 1: Define the customer, pain, result, and risk boundary.

Day 2: Produce 3 to 5 samples, including before/after comparison where possible.

Day 3: Build a one-page offer: deliverables, turnaround time, price, revision limit, and proof.

Day 4: Contact 5 to 10 prospects through the smallest credible channel.

Day 5: Run calls or message interviews. Ask what they currently do, what they dislike, and what they would pay for.

Day 6: Close one paid pilot or collect explicit objections. Do not count compliments as validation.

Day 7: Review evidence and decide: continue, narrow, pivot, or stop.

## Package Template

Use three tiers:

- Trial: one clear output, low price, short turnaround, one revision.
- Standard: fixed bundle, defined inputs, two revisions, delivery checklist.
- Retainer: monthly recurring delivery, content or design calendar, periodic review.

Always include:

- Required customer inputs.
- Delivery format.
- Turnaround time.
- Revision count.
- Human review boundary.
- What is excluded.

## Risk Boundaries

Do not present AI output as professional advice in medical, legal, financial, psychological, or safety-critical contexts unless qualified human review is explicitly part of the workflow.

For personal images, family memories, resumes, interviews, education, pets, and elder-care scenarios, state privacy handling and deletion/storage expectations before accepting materials.

For commercial design and content, check copyright, trademark, portrait rights, platform rules, and whether generated assets are safe for commercial use.

## Retrospective

After 3 pilots, answer:

- Which customer segment responded fastest?
- Which sample caused the strongest buying signal?
- Which step consumed the most human time?
- Which objections repeated?
- Which prompt, checklist, or template should be turned into reusable assets?
- Can the offer move from one-off work to a monthly package?
