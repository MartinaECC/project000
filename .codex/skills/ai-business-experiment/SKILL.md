# AI Business Experiment

## Overview

Use this skill to turn AI monetization ideas into disciplined business experiments. Focus on customer pain, paid validation, deliverable quality, risk boundaries, and reusable operating assets rather than generic "AI can do X" brainstorming.

## Core Workflow

1. Identify the target customer before discussing tools.
2. Define the paid result: what the buyer receives, how they judge it, and why it matters now.
3. Score the opportunity with `references/validation-framework.md`.
4. Design a small sample set that can be made within one day.
5. Create a three-tier offer: trial, standard package, and retainer.
6. Write a 7-day validation plan with prospecting, feedback, paid pilot, and stop/continue criteria.
7. Name risk boundaries: copyright, privacy, platform rules, sensitive advice, quality review, and revision limits.
8. Decide what should become reusable: prompts, intake forms, checklists, scripts, templates, or a new Skill.

## Opportunity Sources

Common sources include:

- Content production: articles, scripts, reviews, industry digests, speeches, private-domain content.
- Visual assets: ecommerce images, posters, thumbnails, packaging, avatars, illustrations, phone-case graphics.
- Local business services: menus, store promotions, local account operations, detail pages, joint campaigns.
- Emotional-value services: pet portraits, memorial products, family memory books, old-photo restoration.
- Education and career services: essays, reading notes, resumes, interviews, family education, learning games.
- Interest-to-commerce services: crafts, beauty, styling, cosplay, journaling, upcycling, tutorial packs.

Treat these as inspiration, not proof. Require market evidence before recommending investment.

## Output Pattern

When evaluating an idea, output in Chinese unless the user asks otherwise:

1. **一句判断**: do / narrow / pause, with the reason.
2. **机会卡**: customer, pain, result, AI leverage, channel, price, risk, proof.
3. **评分**: use the scoring rubric from `references/validation-framework.md`.
4. **样品设计**: 3 to 5 samples with required inputs and review points.
5. **报价包装**: trial, standard, retainer.
6. **7 天验证计划**: daily actions and acceptance criteria.
7. **风险边界**: what not to claim, generate, store, or deliver without review.
8. **Skill 化建议**: reusable prompts, templates, checklists, and whether a new skill is warranted.

## Decision Rules

- Do not count "this is interesting" as validation. Count paid pilots, explicit budget, materials provided, referrals, or repeated urgent objections.
- Prefer ideas with reachable first customers over ideas that require a large audience.
- Prefer visible deliverables over abstract consulting for early validation.
- Prefer service packages that can become recurring retainers.
- Reject or narrow ideas where human review, privacy, or rights clearance cannot be handled.
- If the user already has customers, operations channels, or internal data, adapt the experiment to those assets first.
